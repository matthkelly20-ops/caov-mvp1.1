# Santa Clara MVP Demo Scenario

## Scenario

**University of Portland Baseball at Santa Clara University**

This is the primary MVP demo scenario for CAOV MVP 1.1. It should be used to seed demo data, validate the trip workspace, and test itinerary/trip packet generation.

## Known Trip Details

- Series dates: May 7–9, 2027.
- Arrival: May 6, 2027.
- Departure: May 9, 2027.
- Travel party: 37.
- Day-of-game contact: Henry Muench.
- Uniform colors: all black.
- Flights: United Airlines between PDX and SJC.
- Transportation: charter bus to PDX, throughout the Santa Clara trip, and back to UP campus.
- Practice: May 6, 2027, 7:00–9:00 pm at Santa Clara baseball field.
- Game 1: May 7, 2027 at 6:00 pm.
- Game 2: May 8, 2027 at 6:00 pm.
- Game 3: May 9, 2027 at 12:00 pm.
- Meals: airport lunch on May 6, catered dinner after practice, per diem lunches before night games, catered postgame dinners, airport dinner on return day.
- Budgets: $10 lunch, $20 dinner, hotel breakfast included.
- Roster: based on Portland Pilots baseball roster and staff roster.

## Mock Data Rules

Some demo values may be mock/randomized, including:

- Pass list.
- Guest list.
- Day-of-game cell phone.
- Rooming/eligibility details.
- Placeholder vendors.
- Placeholder confirmation numbers.

All mock/randomized data must be clearly labeled as mock and must not be presented as verified.

## What This Scenario Should Prove

This scenario should prove that CAOV can turn one away series into a structured operations workspace with:

- Game details.
- Travel window.
- Transportation.
- Hotel/lodging.
- Meals/per diem.
- Practice/lift details.
- Host profile.
- Contacts.
- Trip requests/open items.
- Missing inputs.
- Itinerary/trip packet output.

## Open Items To Track

The workspace should be able to track open items such as:

- Final flight numbers and times.
- Hotel details.
- Bus vendor/confirmation.
- Laundry availability and cost.
- Practice confirmation.
- Pregame timing.
- Pass-list process.
- Postgame meal vendors.
- Per diem/funding details.
- Final travel party.
- Rooming list status.

## Product Note

The Santa Clara scenario is not just demo content. It should serve as the forcing function for the MVP data model, trip workspace layout, missing-input checklist, and artifact generation logic.
